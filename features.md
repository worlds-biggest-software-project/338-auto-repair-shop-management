## Cross-Cutting Feature Themes

### Table-Stakes Features

These capabilities are present in nearly every solution and any viable project must match them:

- **Repair order management** — work orders, estimates, invoices, and service history tracking
- **Parts inventory tracking** — stock levels, reorder points, and parts sourcing
- **Payment processing** — online and in-person payment collection with reconciliation
- **Technician and labour tracking** — time tracking and task assignment
- **Basic reporting** — financial summaries, revenue reporting, shop KPIs
- **Customer communication** — estimates/invoices via email/SMS, basic notifications
- **Multi-user support** — role-based access for technicians, advisors, and managers
- **Mobile access** — mobile app or responsive interface for technicians and managers

### Differentiating Features

Capabilities present in some solutions that provide competitive advantage:

- **Digital vehicle inspections (DVIs)** — photo/video capture with customer approvals and condition reports
- **Real-time KPI dashboards** — live business intelligence for profitability and efficiency metrics
- **Advanced customer communication automation** — SMS/email campaigns, live chat, review request workflows
- **Integrated accounting** — built-in general ledger, AR/AP, and tax reporting (vs. QuickBooks sync)
- **Multi-location management** — centralized reporting and team management across branches
- **Configurability and workflow customization** — platforms adaptable to shop-specific processes
- **Kanban-style workflow visualization** — visual task management with drag-and-drop interface
- **Technician performance analytics** — real-time efficiency metrics and coaching insights
- **Fleet-specific features** — specialized support for fleet maintenance and bulk operations

### Underserved Areas / Opportunities

Gaps that represent genuine opportunities for differentiation:

- **AI-assisted diagnostics and repair order generation** — few solutions currently automate DTC code analysis and probable cause suggestions; most still rely on manual service writer input
- **Computer vision analysis of inspection photos** — digital inspections exist but rarely include automated wear assessment and cross-reference to OEM specs
- **Parts demand forecasting** — no solution mentioned emphasizes predictive inventory planning based on vehicle demographics; most use reactive reorder points
- **Integrated recall and TSB monitoring** — while NHTSA recall databases exist, few platforms automate VIN-level checks and customer notification generation
- **Technician scheduling optimization** — real-time capacity planning and shift recommendations based on repair patterns are rarely mentioned
- **Integration with telematics data** — few platforms surface vehicle health data that could trigger preventive maintenance opportunities
- **Advanced accounting analytics** — most platforms emphasize reconciliation over profit margin analysis by service type, technician, or vehicle class
- **Competitor benchmarking** — no platform mentioned provides comparative metrics (e.g., your shop vs. regional average on gross margin, car count, ARO)

### AI-Augmentation Candidates

Manual or rule-based features where AI could provide meaningfully better results:

- **Repair order drafting from symptom descriptions and DTC codes** — AI could suggest probable causes, TSBs, and labour codes, reducing service writer time and improving diagnostic accuracy
- **Inspection photo analysis** — computer vision could automatically flag wear levels, cross-reference to OEM specifications, and generate customer-facing condition reports
- **Technician performance coaching** — NLP analysis of repair patterns could identify training gaps and skill mismatches, triggering targeted development
- **Parts demand forecasting** — time-series forecasting on vehicle age and make mix could optimize inventory, reducing both stockouts and carrying costs
- **Customer churn prediction** — RFM and service history analysis could flag at-risk customers for targeted retention campaigns
- **Dynamic pricing recommendations** — margin analysis by service type and seasonality could inform pricing adjustments
- **Recall and service opportunity identification** — automated NHTSA recall checks combined with vehicle history could proactively surface upsell opportunities

## Legal & IP Summary

All tools analysed are proprietary commercial software with standard SaaS or perpetual licensing models. No open-source solutions were identified in this research, limiting licensing compatibility concerns. However, several platforms require integration with third-party systems (e.g., QuickBooks, PartsTech, MOTOR, CARFAX, AllData) — any project incorporating similar integrations should verify licence compatibility, particularly if the project intends to use GPL or similar copyleft licences.

Payment processing integrations (Stripe, 360Payments) are widely supported; PCI DSS compliance is managed by payment processors in most cases, reducing the project's scope. OEM data access (Mitchell 1, parts catalogs) is proprietary and requires vendor agreements; the project should not attempt to replicate these integrations without explicit vendor licensing.

No patent concerns were identified during research. Digital inspection workflows and kanban-style task management are well-established UX patterns with no apparent IP encumbrances. Recall database monitoring is a standard industry practice and not patented.

## Recommended Feature Scope

Based on the above analysis, a prioritised feature scope for a competitive auto repair shop management platform:

**Must-have (MVP)**
- Repair order management (estimates, work orders, invoices)
- Parts inventory tracking with supplier integrations
- Technician and labour time tracking
- Payment processing (online and in-person)
- Customer communication (estimates/invoices via SMS/email)
- Basic reporting and shop KPIs (revenue, car count, technician hours)
- Mobile technician app for task management

**Should-have (v1.1)**
- Digital vehicle inspections (photo/video with customer approval workflow)
- Real-time KPI dashboards (profitability, efficiency, ARO tracking)
- Multi-location support with centralized reporting
- QuickBooks Online integration for seamless accounting sync
- Advanced customer communication (automated reminders, review campaigns, live chat)
- Workflow customization to accommodate shop-specific processes
- AI-assisted repair order suggestions from DTC codes and symptoms

**Nice-to-have (backlog)**
- Integrated accounting (GL, AR/AP) to eliminate QuickBooks dependency
- Computer vision analysis of inspection photos for automated wear assessment
- Parts demand forecasting based on local vehicle demographics
- NHTSA recall monitoring with automated customer notifications
- Technician performance analytics and coaching recommendations
- Kanban-style workflow visualization for visual task management
- Fleet-specific features (bulk operations, maintenance scheduling)
- Competitor benchmarking and regional performance metrics
